# The Kit — techniques that recur across stages

These are not eight separate methods; they are a handful of techniques, several
of which appear in more than one stage. Reusing them is what makes the finished
assessment read as one intelligence rather than bolted-together subsystems.

- **Gap against an uncontrolled benchmark.** The report's motive force — the
  respondent's answer measured against a fixed point they don't control
  (tenure, a prediction, or another of their own answers). Same move elicits the
  model from the expert: espoused theory gapped against real cases.
  *Used in: [outcome-space](../stages/3-outcome-space.md), [model-induction](../stages/2-model-induction.md), [positioning](../stages/0-positioning.md).*
- **Behavior over self-report.** Ask about observable events, not ratings of
  inner states. Behavior carries its own benchmark and hides its own scoring.
  *Used in: [case-intake](../stages/1-case-intake.md), [instrument](../stages/4-instrument.md).*
- **Recognition over generation.** Experts can't reliably generate their tacit
  model but recognize a right or wrong one instantly. Build for the recognizer:
  seed a draft, harvest the correction.
  *Used in: [model-induction](../stages/2-model-induction.md), [outcome-space](../stages/3-outcome-space.md), [scoring](../stages/5-scoring.md).*
- **Case-grounding (the exemplar test).** One case-set does double duty: it
  accepts the map and calibrates the scoring.
  *Used in: [model-induction](../stages/2-model-induction.md), [scoring](../stages/5-scoring.md).*
- **Author the pairwise.** Interaction cells speak to combinations. Pairwise
  spaces are small and most cells prune before authoring.
  *Used in: [content](../stages/6-content.md).*
- **The three-tense pattern.** Probe a behavior across have-done / doing-now /
  do-consistently. Operationalizes behavioral elicitation and manufactures a
  correlational gap for free.
  *Used in: [instrument](../stages/4-instrument.md) (feeds the gap in [outcome-space](../stages/3-outcome-space.md)).*

## The gap-pattern vocabulary (the join key)

A small, named set of spreads — *proven-but-dormant*, *burst-without-base*,
*consistent-but-shallow* — becomes the single join key across three stages at
once: the [instrument](../stages/4-instrument.md) measures the spread, the
[content](../stages/6-content.md) headlines it, and any ranking is keyed to it.
When the same vocabulary is the spine of the questions, the dot, and the advice,
the assessment feels expert. Name it once and reuse it everywhere.
